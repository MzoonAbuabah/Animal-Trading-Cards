Northwestern Wolf

Northwestern wolves have extremely complex pack organizations. No pack is the same.

  • Scientific name: Canis lupus occidentalis
  • Habitat: United States, Canada, and Alaska
  • Speed: 40 miles per hour
  • Lifespan: Between 6 and 8 years

Northwestern Wolf has a coat of black, white, gray, tan and even blue-ish. Gray or black wolves are the most common color phase found to occur. They typically stand about 30 inches at the shoulder and weigh between 85 and 115 pounds, although they can weigh as much as 145 pounds.
